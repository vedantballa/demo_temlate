DEPARTMENTS

computer engineer

Computer engineering is a field that combines electrical engineering and computer science to develop new technology. Computer engineers design, build, and maintain hardware in modern computers, and focus on integrating hardware and software in a unified system safely and efficiently. They specialize in the design, development, and maintenance of computer systems and networks. Computer engineering involves designing how a computer system works and how it interacts with all its relevant subsystems. Computer engineering majors use computer science, engineering and mathematics to assess problems and design solutions for digital systems.

ENTC engineer

Electronics and telecommunications engineering is a modern engineering discipline that deals with designing, fabricating, producing, testing and supervising the manufacturing process of complex electronic products and systems.

MECHANICAL engineer

Mechanical engineering is the study of physical machines that may involve force and movement. It is an engineering branch that combines engineering physics and mathematics principles with materials science, to design, analyze, manufacture, and maintain mechanical systems.[1] It is one of the oldest and broadest of the engineering branches.

ELECTRONIC engineer

Electronic engineering is a sub-discipline of electrical engineering that emerged in the late-19th century. It is focused on the development of products and systems using electronic technology. An electronics engineer specializes in the design, development, testing, and maintenance of electronic systems, circuits, and components. These engineers apply principles from electrical engineering to create technologies that involve the manipulation of electrical signals for various applications.
